Introduction to HTML

Full Form of HTML: HTML stands for HyperText Markup Language.

What is HTML?
HTML is the standard markup language used to create and design documents on the web. It provides the structure of a webpage by using elements like headings, paragraphs, links, images, tables, and more.

      Uses of HTML:

  • Structuring content on the web.
  • Creating forms of user input.
  • Embedding multimedia like images, videos, and audio.
  • Creating hyperlinks to navigate between webpages.

HTML Elements: are the building blocks of HTML documents. An HTML element consists of a start tag, content, and an end tag. HTML elements can represent a variety of content types like text, images, links, forms, etc.

      Why these fruits are healthy:

      Orange:
Oranges are rich in vitamin C, which boosts the immune system and helps maintain healthy skin. They also contain antioxidants, which protect cells from damage and support heart health. The high fiber content aids in digestion and helps regulate blood sugar levels.
      Banana:
Bananas are rich in potassium, which is essential for maintaining healthy blood pressure and proper muscle function. They also provide vitamin B6, which aids brain function and supports the nervous system. Bananas are a great source of natural energy due to their carbohydrate content.
      Pineapple:
Pineapples are a great source of vitamin C, supporting immune function and skin health. They contain bromelain, an enzyme that helps with digestion and reduces inflammation. Pineapples also have a high water content, aiding hydration and supporting digestive health.
      Grapes:
Grapes are full of antioxidants, particularly resveratrol, which helps protect against heart disease and aging. They are also a good source of vitamins C and K, promoting a healthy immune system and bone health. Grapes have high water content, which helps in hydration and supports skin health.
      Apple:
Apples are packed with dietary fiber, which promotes good digestion and helps manage weight. They are also a good source of antioxidants, such as flavonoids, which reduce inflammation and lower the risk of chronic diseases like heart disease. Additionally, apples help in maintaining healthy blood sugar levels.
